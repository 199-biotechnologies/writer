### Ablation Results (Step 10)
- **Ran:** 3 formats × 2 modes × 10 prompts × 2 seeds = 120 generations, ~2 hours with persistent worker
- **Winner: `chat + wrapped`** (0.567 distance, 1.6% off-domain leakage, 0 failures)
- **Key finding: format is not the bottleneck.** `text` format gets best distance (0.332) but 12% leakage — eliminated at gate 1
- **`text + raw` produces questions/exclamations** (36.4 Q/1k, 10.9 !/1k) but leaks too much (8.6% off-domain)
- **Raw inference broken for chat/completions adapters** — 45% failure rate (chat+raw), 25% (completions+raw)

## Key Decisions Made

1. **Chat format wins by default, not by excellence.** It's the safest (lowest leakage, no failures) but structurally flat (0.0 exclamations). The real bottleneck is training quality (100 steps, loss 2.54 — not enough to learn when to stop repeating).

2. **Ablation adapters are 100-step quickfire.** They produce degenerate outputs (repetition collapse). The production adapter at `~/Library/Application Support/writer/profiles/default/adapters/` was trained for 500 steps (loss 1.80) and is much better. All ablation tests used the 100-step adapters in `ablation_results/workspace/`.

3. **GPT Pro recommended: "If chat still wins, stop treating format as the bottleneck. Next task is better scoring, better candidate generation, and later DPO."**

4. **Codex caught 7 issues across 2 review rounds.** All fixed. Key ones: gate logic wasn't truly lexicographic, voice gate rewarded excess punctuation instead of closeness, empty buckets could win, Unicode boundary matching was ASCII-only.

5. **GPT Pro found 3 inconsistent slop implementations.** Unified to one canonical `compute_slop_score()` in scoring.rs. Author-valid words now excluded consistently across scoring, filter, logit_bias, and system prompt.

6. **Sentence segmentation is confirmed broken** — abbreviation periods create false short sentences. Adams mean 6.8 is likely deflated. EMD histogram shows 22% of sentences in 1-2 word bin. Fix deferred — needs custom splitter.

7. **Persistent worker saves ~10s per generation.** The ablation would have taken ~6 hours without it; took ~2 hours with it. But model still takes ~45s per generation on 26B.

## Gotchas & Warnings

1. **The ablation adapters are 100-step quickfire** — they produce degenerate repetitive text. Do NOT use them for production. The production adapter at `~/Library/Application Support/writer/profiles/default/adapters/` is the 500-step one.
2. **Gemma 4 26B is a thinking model.** Empty content = ran out of tokens during thinking. `max_tokens >= 4096` required.
3. **mlx-lm 0.31.2 is required** for Gemma 4 support. 0.31.1 throws `Model type gemma4 not supported`.
4. **The logits_processors kwarg is `logits_processors` (plural)** — not `logits_processor`. This broke the first ablation run.
5. **The persistent worker must be used per-adapter.** Switching adapters requires a new worker. The ablation command handles this automatically.
6. **`f64::MAX` appears in JSON** for all-failure buckets. This is intentional — prevents empty combos from winning gates.
7. **Sentence segmentation is broken** — `unicode_segmentation::unicode_sentences()` splits on abbreviation periods. The histogram shows 22% 1-2 word sentences which is inflated. Fix this before trusting EMD scores.
8. **Single-agent sessions: do NOT use TaskCreate/TaskUpdate.** Global CLAUDE.md rule.
9. **User does NOT want a rewrite system.** The product is a generative model that writes in the target voice from any prompt.
10. **Repetition penalty 1.3 is a band-aid.** The real fix is better training (more steps, better data, eventually DPO).
11. **`ablation_results/` is gitignored** — the 2GB+ adapter safetensors are local only.
